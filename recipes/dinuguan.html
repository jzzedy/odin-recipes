Dinuguan Recipe

Dinuguan, also known as "chocolate meat", is a savory Filipino stew made with pork, pork blood, and spices. It's known for its rich, bold flavor and dark, slightly thick gravy. The dish often includes pork offal like intestines, ears, and other parts, along with garlic, onions, vinegar, and chilies. It's a popular dish, often served with steamed rice or puto (Filipino rice cake).

Ingredients

  • Pork
  • Pork Blood
  • Vinegar
  • Garlic
  • Onions

Steps

  1. Saut√© garlic, onions, and ginger in oil until softened.
  2. Add the pork and cook until browned.
  3. Add vinegar, water, and other seasonings like bay leaves and fish sauce. Simmer until the pork is tender.
  4. Stir in the pork blood, ensuring it's evenly distributed and doesn't clump.
  5. Add sugar, salt, and pepper to taste. Simmer for a few more minutes, then serve hot.
